Understanding Spectrum Distribution in RF & Frequency Bands Used in FDD and TDD

✅ What is Spectrum in RF?

In Radio Frequency (RF) communication, spectrum refers to the range of electromagnetic frequencies used to transmit wireless signals. It's essentially the "invisible highway" over which all wireless communication travels.

✅ Spectrum Distribution in RF

The RF spectrum spans from 3 kHz to 300 GHz and is divided into different bands:

Band Name Frequency Range Common Uses
LF30 – 300 kHzNavigation, AM broadcasting
MF300 kHz – 3 MHzAM Radio
HF3 – 30 MHzShortwave, Amateur Radio
VHF30 – 300 MHzFM Radio, TV, Air Traffic
UHF300 MHz – 3 GHzMobile phones, Wi-Fi, GPS
SHF3 – 30 GHz5G, Satellite, Radar
EHF30 – 300 GHzAdvanced Radar, mmWave 5G
